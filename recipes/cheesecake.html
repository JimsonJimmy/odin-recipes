Cheesecake

Description

Cheesecake is a rich, creamy dessert made from a smooth mixture of cream cheese, sugar, and eggs, all baked over a buttery biscuit or graham cracker crust. This indulgent treat comes in many varieties, from classic New York-style to fruit-topped, chocolate, and even no-bake versions. The texture ranges from dense and velvety to light and fluffy, depending on the recipe. Often finished with a luscious glaze, fresh fruit, or a drizzle of caramel or chocolate, cheesecake is a favorite for special occasions or a delightful sweet treat any time!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup melted butter

For the Filling:

  • 2 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup sour cream (optional, for extra creaminess)
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (optional, for stability)

For the Topping (Optional):

  • Fresh fruit (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Whipped cream
  • Caramel or chocolate drizzle

Cheesecake Recipe

  • Step 1: Prepare the Crust

    • Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
    • In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs and sugar.
    • Add melted butter and stir until the mixture resembles wet sand.
    • Press the mixture into the bottom of a springform pan to form the crust.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 10 minutes, then remove and set aside to cool.

  • Step 2: Prepare the Filling

    • In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy.
    • Add granulated sugar and continue to beat until well combined.
    • One at a time, add the eggs, beating after each addition.
    • Stir in the vanilla extract and, if using, the sour cream and flour.
    • Pour the cream cheese filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan.

  • Step 3: Bake the Cheesecake

    • Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for 50–60 minutes, or until the center is set.
    • If the cheesecake begins to brown on top, cover with foil for the last 15 minutes of baking.
    • Allow the cheesecake to cool in the oven with the door slightly ajar for 1 hour.

  • Step 4: Chill the Cheesecake

    • Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool at room temperature for another hour.
    • Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results.

  • Step 5: Add the Toppings (Optional)

    • Top the cheesecake with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or drizzle with caramel or chocolate sauce.

  • Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

    • Once chilled and topped, slice the cheesecake and enjoy a delicious homemade treat!
